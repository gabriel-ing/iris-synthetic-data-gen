Agent Action Policy, Safety Controls, and Audit Logging

This guidance governs when and how an agent may write to IRIS for card and dispute operations. Work in two phases: plan (gather evidence, decide, preview) and execute (apply the smallest safe change, verify, log). Keep the language in audit notes plain and consistent. If something feels like a “close call,” stop and escalate before writing.

Two‑phase workflow every time
1) Plan the change.
- Validate the target row(s) exist and you’ve got the right CustomerId/CardId/MerchantId/TransactionId.
- Pull relevant history from ModestExpectationsCaptial.Transactions for the CardId or MerchantId, including Status (APPROVED, DECLINED, REFUNDED, REVERSED), Channel (ECOM or POS), EntryMode (MANUAL, CHIP, TAP), DeclineReason (INSUFFICIENT_FUNDS, SUSPECTED_FRAUD, LIMIT_EXCEEDED, INVALID_CVV), CardPresent, and any IsFraud flags.
- For disputes, confirm the TransactionId is posted (i.e., has PostedAt) and that there is no active Disputes.State of OPEN or UNDER_REVIEW already.
- Sanity‑check Merchants.RiskTier (LOW, MED, HIGH) and category context (GROCERY, DIGITAL, RETAIL, FUEL, TRAVEL, DINING) if you’re considering a risk change.
- Draft your intended writes with explicit before/after values: Cards.Status (ACTIVE, BLOCKED, CLOSED) and Cards.ClosedAt; Disputes insert with ReasonCode (DUPLICATE, FRAUD, NOT_AS_DESCRIBED, NOT_RECEIVED, CANCELLED) and State (OPEN, UNDER_REVIEW, RESOLVED); Disputes.Outcome (CUSTOMER_WON, MERCHANT_WON, WITHDRAWN, CHARGEBACK) and Disputes.ResolvedAt; Merchants.RiskTier.
- Write the audit note draft before you execute.

2) Execute the change.
- Execute only the agreed fields in one transaction: write:Cards.Status, write:Cards.ClosedAt, write:Disputes(insert), write:Disputes.State, write:Disputes.Outcome, write:Disputes.ResolvedAt, write:Merchants.RiskTier.
- Immediately re‑query the changed row(s) to confirm the new value.
- Post the final audit note with the exact values changed and the verification query result.

Decision rules and allowed actions

Blocking a card (freeze/lock)
- You may set Cards.Status to BLOCKED when you have one of these:
  - Two or more APPROVED ECOM transactions in the last 24 hours marked IsFraud = 1 for the same CardId; or
  - Three or more rapid‑fire DECLINED attempts with DeclineReason = SUSPECTED_FRAUD or INVALID_CVV across mixed EntryMode (e.g., MANUAL and TAP/contactless) in the last 2 hours; or
  - A customer report of unauthorized use corroborated by mismatched Channel (customer is traveling POS only, activity is ECOM).
- If any of the above is true, set Cards.Status = BLOCKED. Do not set CLOSED at this stage. No other card fields should be changed.
- Record the evidence in the audit note (TransactionId list, timestamps, DeclineReason values, and the IRIS SELECT you ran).

Closing a card
- Golden answer: Do not set Cards.Status = CLOSED automatically under normal circumstances.
- You may set Cards.Status = CLOSED and Cards.ClosedAt = current timestamp only if all are true:
  - The card is already BLOCKED and has been for at least 30 days; and
  - There have been zero APPROVED or REFUNDED transactions on this CardId in the last 30 days; and
  - The same CustomerId has a different card with Cards.Status = ACTIVE and OpenedAt > the blocked card’s OpenedAt (i.e., a replacement exists).
- Otherwise, escalate to a supervisor for approval before closing. Never close a card solely due to DECLINED activity, LIMIT_EXCEEDED, or INSUFFICIENT_FUNDS.

Opening and resolving disputes (dispute/chargeback/claim)
- You may insert into ModestExpectationsCaptial.Disputes when a posted transaction is contested and no active dispute exists for that TransactionId.
- Allowed ReasonCode values are DUPLICATE, FRAUD, NOT_AS_DESCRIBED, NOT_RECEIVED, or CANCELLED. Start with State = OPEN.
- When you begin analysis, you may set Disputes.State = UNDER_REVIEW.
- To resolve, you must set Disputes.Outcome to one of CUSTOMER_WON, MERCHANT_WON, WITHDRAWN, or CHARGEBACK, set Disputes.ResolvedAt = current timestamp, and set Disputes.State = RESOLVED in the same transaction.
- If you select Outcome = CHARGEBACK, confirm the transaction is not already REVERSED or fully REFUNDED. If it is, choose WITHDRAWN or CUSTOMER_WON as appropriate and note why (refund vs reversal/void).
- Do not edit Transactions.Status, Amount, or IsFraud as part of dispute handling.

Merchant risk tier changes (risk score/tiering/blocklisting)
- You may update Merchants.RiskTier to LOW, MED, or HIGH when transaction evidence supports a change:
  - Raise to HIGH if the merchant has three or more Disputes with ReasonCode = FRAUD or DUPLICATE in the last 7 days and at least one Outcome = CHARGEBACK.
  - Lower to LOW only if there have been zero Disputes opened in the last 30 days and the recent volume is stable (Transactions count within ±20% of the 30‑day average) and no Outcomes = CHARGEBACK in the same period.
- If the merchant is in Category = GROCERY or FUEL and most activity is POS with EntryMode = CHIP/TAP, favor MED rather than HIGH unless Outcomes include CHARGEBACK. Add nuance, not overreaction.
- Do not mass‑update multiple merchants in one transaction. One MerchantId per change.

Additional constraints and “don’ts”
- Do not set Cards.ClosedAt unless also setting Cards.Status = CLOSED (same transaction).
- Do not set Disputes.ResolvedAt unless also setting Disputes.State = RESOLVED and Disputes.Outcome to a final value.
- Do not backdate Disputes.ResolvedAt earlier than Disputes.OpenedAt.
- Do not change Customers.Status, Transactions fields, or any non‑allowed columns as part of this SOP.
- If you’re unsure whether a contactless “tap” was card‑present, check Transactions.CardPresent and EntryMode = TAP; do not infer from MerchantName alone.

Confidence and thresholds before writing
- Minimum signal to block: confidence ≥ 0.8 that activity is unauthorized. Practically, that means corroboration by either IsFraud = 1 on APPROVED events or a pattern of SUSPECTED_FRAUD/INVALID_CVV declines across Channels (ECOM and POS) or EntryModes (MANUAL and TAP).
- Minimum signal to close: confidence ≥ 0.95 that the card is replaced and dormant as defined above.
- Minimum signal to set Merchants.RiskTier = HIGH: at least one recent CHARGEBACK outcome plus a multi‑day pattern, not a single day spike.

Rollback guidance
- If you blocked a card in error, and no irreversible outcomes were created, set Cards.Status back to ACTIVE. Do not touch ClosedAt.
- If you closed a card prematurely, set Cards.Status back to BLOCKED or ACTIVE per supervisor guidance and set Cards.ClosedAt = NULL in the same transaction. Document the reason in the audit note.
- If you set the wrong Merchants.RiskTier, change it back to the prior value and capture the correction in the audit note with the supporting query.
- If you resolved a dispute with the wrong Outcome, update Disputes.Outcome to the correct final value and ensure Disputes.State remains RESOLVED. Do not unset ResolvedAt; instead, add a new audit note explaining the correction timestamp.

What to write in the audit note
Every write must have a contemporaneous audit note. Use short, factual sentences:
- Trigger: what prompted the action (fraud spree, customer call, system alert).
- Evidence: query summaries, key field values (Transactions.Status, DeclineReason, EntryMode, Channel; Disputes.State/Outcome; Cards.Status).
- Decision: the if/then rule you applied.
- Action: exact table.field changes with before → after values and timestamps (e.g., Cards.Status: ACTIVE → BLOCKED; Disputes.ResolvedAt: null → 2026‑03‑03T14:05Z).
- Impact and next steps: customer comms, monitoring, follow‑ups.

Example audit note (free text)
“2026‑03‑03 14:05Z, Agent JS. Trigger: Fraud alert and customer confirmed unauthorized ECOM charges. Evidence: CardId 8842 had 3 APPROVED ECOM transactions (IsFraud=1) at Merchant ‘QuickApps’ between 12:10–12:32Z; 5 additional DECLINED attempts with DeclineReason=SUSPECTED_FRAUD via EntryMode=MANUAL and TAP. Decision: If ≥2 APPROVED IsFraud + mixed EntryMode fraud, then BLOCK card. Action: ModestExpectationsCaptial.Cards.Status ACTIVE → BLOCKED at 14:03Z. Created Dispute for TransactionId 991177 (ReasonCode=FRAUD, State=OPEN). Verification: post‑write SELECT shows Cards.Status=BLOCKED. Impact: customer notified; monitor for new attempts for 48h.”

Common pitfalls
- Mixing up REFUNDED vs REVERSED (void). If a transaction is REVERSED, do not pursue CHARGEBACK; choose WITHDRAWN with rationale. If REFUNDED, only pursue a dispute if refund < DisputedAmount and note the delta.
- Forgetting to set Disputes.ResolvedAt when you set Outcome and State=RESOLVED.
- Closing a card without a replacement ACTIVE card for the same CustomerId.
- Lowering Merchants.RiskTier immediately after a volume drop without checking Outcomes in the last 30 days.
- Using “contactless” as proof of fraud without checking CardPresent and EntryMode = TAP.

Step‑by‑step examples

Scenario 1: Block a card and open a fraud dispute
You see a burst of unauthorized e‑commerce purchases (user said “online charges,” i.e., ECOM) at a DIGITAL merchant, plus declines with SUSPECTED_FRAUD. You SELECT from Transactions for CardId 8842 and confirm three APPROVED ECOM transactions marked IsFraud=1 and five DECLINED attempts across MANUAL and TAP. If/then rule triggers: set Cards.Status = BLOCKED. You INSERT into Disputes with TransactionId of the largest unauthorized charge, ReasonCode = FRAUD, State = OPEN, DisputedAmount = Amount. You write the audit note, verify the card now shows BLOCKED, and schedule a replacement card (outside IRIS) while monitoring for more POS attempts.

Scenario 2: Raise a merchant’s risk tier after chargebacks
Over the past week, MerchantId 552 shows five new disputes on POS chip transactions (CHIP), three resolved with Outcome = CHARGEBACK, one WITHDRAWN, one pending UNDER_REVIEW. SELECT confirms the merchant is Category = RETAIL with current Merchants.RiskTier = MED and Country = US. If/then rule: if ≥1 CHARGEBACK and ≥3 disputes in 7 days, raise to HIGH. You update Merchants.RiskTier MED → HIGH, verify the write, and note that many transactions were approved then reversed after tap/contactless retries. You document the change in the audit note and flag for the risk team to consider acquirer monitoring.

Searching and alternate phrasing you might hear
- “Freeze” or “lock” the card means set Cards.Status to BLOCKED. Some callers say “cancel” when they mean BLOCKED; “cancel/close” should map to CLOSED only with the closure rules above.
- “Contactless,” “tap,” or “NFC” maps to EntryMode = TAP; in‑store or “card‑present” maps to POS with CHIP/TAP.
- “Online,” “web,” “e‑commerce,” or “ECOM” all map to Transactions.Channel = ECOM.
- “Dispute,” “chargeback,” “claim,” or “case” all map to Disputes records with ReasonCode and State/Outcome.
- “Void” is typically REVERSED; “refund” maps to REFUNDED.
- “PAN token,” “token,” or “CardToken” refers to Cards.CardToken.

Quick field reminders while you work
- Cards.Status must be one of ACTIVE, BLOCKED, CLOSED. If CLOSED, set Cards.ClosedAt at the same time.
- Disputes.State moves OPEN → UNDER_REVIEW → RESOLVED; when RESOLVED, set Disputes.Outcome and Disputes.ResolvedAt.
- Merchants.RiskTier can be LOW, MED, or HIGH; change one merchant at a time with clear evidence.

Verification after any write
- Re‑query the exact row you updated (by primary key) and read back the changed field(s).
- For card changes, scan last 24h Transactions for the CardId to ensure no new APPROVED activity slipped through post‑block.
- For disputes, verify no second active dispute exists for the same TransactionId.
- For merchant risk changes, confirm the new RiskTier reads back as expected and note any immediate shift in declines due to SUSPECTED_FRAUD (monitoring only; no edits to Transactions).

Escalate when in doubt
- Any closure that doesn’t meet the three closure conditions.
- Any merchant downgrade to LOW within 30 days of any CHARGEBACK.
- Any case where the audit note would rely on “assumed” behavior without direct field evidence.

By following this plan‑then‑execute approach, using the exact allowed values, and writing crisp audit notes, we keep changes safe, reversible, and easy to review.
