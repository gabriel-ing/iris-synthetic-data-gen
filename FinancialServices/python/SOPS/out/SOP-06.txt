Fraud Triage and Containment (Rule-Based)

This playbook guides quick investigation and containment when a card shows suspected fraud based on Transactions.IsFraud flags, velocity bursts, out-of-segment behavior, and merchant risk/category patterns. It is written for analysts who can query IRIS SQL and who may take limited actions on cards.

First look: gather the facts you need
- Identify the card and customer. Pull the card row from ModestExpectationsCaptial.Cards using CardId, and join to ModestExpectationsCaptial.Customers on CustomerId. Confirm Cards.Status (ACTIVE, BLOCKED, or CLOSED) and Cards.CreditLimit.
- Pull the past 48 hours of ModestExpectationsCaptial.Transactions for this CardId. Only consider Transactions.Status = APPROVED for containment decisions; note that other valid values are DECLINED, REFUNDED, and REVERSED.
- Join to ModestExpectationsCaptial.Merchants by MerchantId to get Merchants.RiskTier (LOW, MED, HIGH), Merchants.Category (GROCERY, DIGITAL, RETAIL, FUEL, TRAVEL, DINING), and Country.
- Check for any related ModestExpectationsCaptial.Disputes on those transactions, especially Disputes.State in OPEN or UNDER_REVIEW and Disputes.ReasonCode of FRAUD, DUPLICATE, NOT_AS_DESCRIBED, NOT_RECEIVED, or CANCELLED. If a dispute already ended with Outcome CHARGEBACK, note it as prior confirmed fraud.

Helpful IRIS SQL snippets (adapt as needed)
- Last 48h APPROVED by card:
  select t.* from ModestExpectationsCaptial.Transactions t
  where t.CardId = :cardId and t.Status = 'APPROVED' and t.AuthAt >= DATEADD('hour', -48, CURRENT_TIMESTAMP)
  order by t.AuthAt desc
- With merchant risk and category:
  select t.TransactionId, t.AuthAt, t.Amount, t.Currency, t.Channel, t.EntryMode, t.CardPresent, t.IsFraud,
         m.MerchantName, m.RiskTier, m.Category, m.Country
  from ModestExpectationsCaptial.Transactions t
  join ModestExpectationsCaptial.Merchants m on t.MerchantId = m.MerchantId
  where t.CardId = :cardId and t.Status = 'APPROVED' and t.AuthAt >= DATEADD('hour', -48, CURRENT_TIMESTAMP)
- Baseline behavior (last 30d averages) by channel:
  select t.Channel, count(*) as txn_cnt, sum(t.Amount) as amt
  from ModestExpectationsCaptial.Transactions t
  where t.CardId = :cardId and t.Status = 'APPROVED' and t.AuthAt >= DATEADD('day', -30, CURRENT_TIMESTAMP)
  group by t.Channel

What to look for (signals)
- IsFraud signals: If Transactions.IsFraud = 1 on any APPROVED transaction, treat that as a primary indicator; two or more such flags in a short window strongly points to fraud.
- Velocity bursts: Rapid-fire spends are classic. Count APPROVED transactions in the last 10 minutes, 1 hour, and 24 hours. Distinguish by Transactions.Channel (ECOM vs POS) and EntryMode (MANUAL, CHIP, TAP). “Tap” and “contactless” both map to EntryMode = TAP; “keyed entry” or “card-not-present” (CNP) typically show as MANUAL.
- Out-of-segment behavior: Check ModestExpectationsCaptial.Customers for Segment and multipliers: SegmentTxnMultiplier, SegmentAmountMultiplier, SegmentEcomMultiplier, SegmentDeclineMultiplier, SegmentDisputeMultiplier. Compare the last 24 hours vs the last-30-day average approvals. If the last 24h count exceeds average × SegmentTxnMultiplier, that’s a signal. For e-commerce, tighten by SegmentEcomMultiplier.
- Merchant patterns: Clusters at Merchants.RiskTier = HIGH or MED, especially in Merchants.Category DIGITAL or TRAVEL, are higher risk than LOW risk tier GROCERY or FUEL. Multiple different high-risk merchants in minutes can indicate testing by a fraud ring or account takeover (ATO).
- Geography: If Merchants.Country deviates from Customers.Region (e.g., customer region “US-East” and merchants in multiple non-US countries in an hour), treat cautiously. A sudden foreign POS spree while CardPresent = true may suggest skimming or lost/stolen; a foreign ECOM run (CNP) maps to ATO more often.
- Declines: A series of Transactions.Status = DECLINED with DeclineReason like SUSPECTED_FRAUD, INVALID_CVV, or LIMIT_EXCEEDED followed by quick APPROVED attempts can be part of probing. Use declines as context; do not block on declines alone.

If / then decision rules (evidence-based)
Block immediately (set Cards.Status to BLOCKED) if any of the following are all true:
- The card is currently ACTIVE, and
- Evidence threshold met:
  - Two or more APPROVED transactions with IsFraud = 1 within 15 minutes, and at least one merchant with RiskTier MED or HIGH; or
  - Five or more APPROVED ECOM transactions within 10 minutes with EntryMode = MANUAL (keyed/CNP), regardless of merchant, and total Amount ≥ average daily approved amount × SegmentAmountMultiplier; or
  - A single APPROVED ECOM transaction with EntryMode = MANUAL that is ≥ 80% of Cards.CreditLimit (or ≥ 3× the 30-day average ticket) combined with a prior OPEN dispute in the last 14 days on the same card with ReasonCode = FRAUD; or
  - Three or more APPROVED transactions at Merchants.Category in DIGITAL or TRAVEL, from two or more different MerchantId, totaling ≥ $300 (or local equivalent) within 30 minutes, with CardPresent = false and Customers.Region not matching Merchants.Country for any of them.

Recommend monitoring (do not block yet) if:
- Velocity is elevated but within SegmentTxnMultiplier and SegmentEcomMultiplier adjusted norms, or
- Activity is at Merchants.RiskTier = LOW (e.g., GROCERY, FUEL) near the customer’s usual region, and
- IsFraud = 0 on recent approvals, and there are no OPEN/UNDER_REVIEW disputes with ReasonCode FRAUD.

Recommend escalation to fraud operations lead if:
- Exposure is high: any single APPROVED amount ≥ 50% of Cards.CreditLimit or total last-24h APPROVED ≥ 2× average daily, but evidence for immediate block is borderline; or
- You observe linked patterns across multiple Cards for the same Customers.CustomerId (“bust-out”), or across multiple customers at the same HIGH risk merchant (“fraud ring”); or
- There is a mix of card-present (TAP/CHIP) and CNP (ECOM/MANUAL) across distant geographies in hours, suggesting sophisticated ATO/skimming.

How to execute allowed actions
- Allowed: write:Cards.Status
  - You may set Cards.Status from ACTIVE to BLOCKED when one of the “Block immediately” conditions is met. Use a targeted update:
    update ModestExpectationsCaptial.Cards
    set Status = 'BLOCKED'
    where CardId = :cardId and Status = 'ACTIVE'
  - Before writing, capture evidence: the TransactionId(s), AuthAt times, IsFraud flags, Merchants.RiskTier/Category, Channel, EntryMode, CardPresent, and any Disputes.State/ReasonCode context.
- Not allowed without escalation:
  - Do not set Cards.Status = CLOSED. Closure requires customer confirmation or a card reissue workflow outside this SOP.
  - Do not modify Transactions rows (Status, DeclineReason, IsFraud) or Disputes rows. You may read only.
- Allowed: recommend:monitoring
  - If monitoring is appropriate, note it in the audit and notify the monitoring queue with the observed signals (e.g., “elevated ECOM velocity, within segment multipliers”).
- Allowed: recommend:escalate
  - If criteria above are met, include a concise summary and tag for lead review.

Evidence thresholds and baselines (practical tips)
- To compute a daily baseline, take last-30-day APPROVED count and amount by channel. Multiply by SegmentTxnMultiplier for count, SegmentAmountMultiplier for amounts. For ECOM-only analysis, also apply SegmentEcomMultiplier. Example: if 30-day average is 3 ECOM/day and SegmentEcomMultiplier is 1.5, treat >5 ECOM in 24h as elevated.
- Consider Transactions.EntryMode carefully: MANUAL often indicates CNP; CHIP and TAP imply card-present. Contactless “tap” is TAP. If CardPresent = false and Channel = POS, recheck joins and data validity.
- Track declines contextually: repeated DECLINED with SUSPECTED_FRAUD followed by small APPROVEDs can be tests before a larger hit.

Scenarios

Scenario 1: ECOM manual burst with IsFraud flags
A card with Status = ACTIVE shows three APPROVED ECOM charges in 12 minutes at two DIGITAL merchants. EntryMode = MANUAL on all three, CardPresent = false. Transactions.IsFraud = 1 on the first two; the third is unflagged but at a new MerchantId with RiskTier = HIGH. The last 30-day average for ECOM is 1/day; today’s three exceed SegmentEcomMultiplier × baseline. You set Cards.Status to BLOCKED, document TransactionId, AuthAt, MerchantName, RiskTier, and the IsFraud flags, and recommend escalation because total exposure is 65% of Cards.CreditLimit.

Scenario 2: Local POS tap pattern, low risk
A customer’s card shows two APPROVED POS transactions: one at GROCERY and one at FUEL, both TAP within 30 minutes and within the customer’s usual region. Merchants.RiskTier = LOW for both. There’s a DECLINED earlier with DeclineReason = INVALID_CVV on an ECOM attempt, but no IsFraud flags and no OPEN disputes. Velocity is slightly elevated but within SegmentTxnMultiplier. You do not block; you recommend monitoring for 24 hours and note the benign merchant categories and local pattern.

Common pitfalls to avoid
- Blocking on DECLINED or REFUNDED transactions. Only act on APPROVED. Don’t confuse a REVERSED auth with a posted spend.
- Ignoring time zones in AuthAt; always use server time consistently when defining “last 10 minutes” or “last 24 hours.”
- Joining Merchants on MerchantName instead of MerchantId, which can merge unrelated stores and distort risk tiers.
- Treating TAP as e-commerce; TAP is card-present contactless, not CNP.
- Overweighting single LOW risk tier approvals (e.g., GROCERY) as fraud just because of a prior decline; look for clusters and context.
- Forgetting to check current Cards.Status; attempting to block an already BLOCKED or CLOSED card adds noise.

What to write in the audit note
Keep it concise and evidence-driven. Reference the exact rows and fields you used, the rule triggered, and the action. Include CardId, key TransactionId(s), timestamps, Channels, EntryModes, merchant risk details, and any Disputes context.

Example audit note:
“CardId 9241 blocked due to ECOM manual burst. 3 APPROVED in 12m: Txn 557812 (AuthAt 2026-03-03T14:12Z, $120, ECOM, MANUAL, CardPresent=false, IsFraud=1, Merchant ‘FastPlay’, RiskTier=HIGH, Category=DIGITAL), Txn 557845 (14:18Z, $85, IsFraud=1, same merchant), Txn 557901 (14:24Z, $410, new MerchantId 7781, RiskTier=MED, Category=TRAVEL). 30d ECOM avg 1/day; today=3 > SegmentEcomMultiplier threshold. No prior RESOLVED disputes; 1 OPEN dispute (ReasonCode=FRAUD) on 2026-02-25. Action: set Cards.Status from ACTIVE to BLOCKED. Recommend escalate due to 46% CreditLimit exposure.”

Answering the two golden questions
- Should we block immediately? Yes, if the “Block immediately” conditions are met: multiple IsFraud=1 approvals in minutes, high-velocity ECOM with MANUAL entry beyond segment-adjusted baselines, high-risk merchants cluster, or recent FRAUD dispute plus high-value CNP approval. Otherwise, monitor or escalate per the criteria.
- What’s the evidence that this is fraud vs legitimate activity? Evidence includes IsFraud flags on APPROVED transactions, velocity beyond SegmentTxnMultiplier/SegmentEcomMultiplier, CNP patterns (ECOM + MANUAL) with CardPresent = false, clustering at Merchants.RiskTier MED/HIGH and Categories DIGITAL/TRAVEL, mismatched geography, and recent Disputes.State OPEN/UNDER_REVIEW with ReasonCode FRAUD. Legitimate patterns tend to be POS with CHIP/TAP at LOW risk categories (GROCERY, FUEL) near the customer’s region, without IsFraud flags and within segment baselines.

Retrieval hooks and alternate phrasing embedded
Analysts may search for “chargeback” when they mean Disputes.Outcome CHARGEBACK, or “dispute” for Disputes.State OPEN/UNDER_REVIEW. “Card-not-present” and “CNP” map to ECOM with EntryMode = MANUAL. “Tap” or “contactless” map to EntryMode = TAP. “Velocity” or “rapid fire” refer to counts in short windows. “Account takeover” or “ATO,” “friendly fraud,” “fraud ring,” and “bust-out” are patterns inferred from clusters across merchants, channels, and customers. “Skimming” can appear as POS CHIP/TAP in unexpected geographies with CardPresent = true.

Final reminders
- Take action only on solid evidence documented from the database: cite Transactions.IsFraud, AuthAt, Amount, Channel, EntryMode, CardPresent; Merchants.RiskTier and Category; Cards.Status; and any Disputes.ReasonCode/State. When in doubt, recommend:escalate rather than over-block.
- After blocking, hand off for reissue outside this SOP. Keep your audit note crisp; it’s the anchor for downstream decisions and any later chargeback or dispute workflows.
